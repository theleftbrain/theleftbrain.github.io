Public university tuition has sky-rocketed since 1980. Wages haven't.

Public university tuition has sky-rocketed since 1980. Wages haven't.

@DomRussel - May 21, 2016

From 1980 until 2013 tuition for public universities rapidly increased. At the same time, however, the median worker's wages stagnated. As the map below shows, while tuition at flagship public universities has increased by over 200% in most states, median incomes have rarely increased by more than 15% (all percentages are after adjusting for inflation for both wages and tuition). Mouse-over or click on the states below to see how this difference between change in tuition and change in median wages have affected residents in all 50 U.S. states.


Tuition data comes from the National Center of Education Statistics' IPEDS database. Income data comes from the U.S. Census Bureau. This is the google sheet with the data used for this visualization.
